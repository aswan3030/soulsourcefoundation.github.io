About Us

History

Soul Source is active in promoting the empowerment and education of women and girls in Western Kenya. In early 2000, Dr. Elaine Millam, Rev. Carleton Peterson, Dennis Coyne, and Eileen Page began discussions on how they might help all women and girls world-wide to live full and productive lives. In 2008, a year after Peterson’s death from cancer and in memory of him, Dr. Millam founded the organization as a 501c3 non-profit with Kenya as the focus of the organization. Prof. Theodora Ayot of North Park University was a key participant in giving direction to Soul Source as she lives her commitment of giving back to her people on Rusinga Island where she was born and raised in Kenya’s northwestern corner of Lake Victoria. Active engagement in programming for Women's Empowerment programs and educational support for young women in secondary school, college and university, and nursery school in communities in the region became the goals of Soul Source.
